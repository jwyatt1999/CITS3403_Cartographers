Rules


Official Rules for Cartographers: A Roll Player Tale by Thunderworks Games

Introduction

Queen Gimnax has ordered the reclamation of the northern lands. As a cartographer in her service, you are sent to map this territory, claiming it for the Kindgom of Nalos. Through official edicts, the queen announces which lands she prizes most, and you will increase your reputation by meeting her demands.

But you are not alone in this wilderness. The Dragul contest your claims with their outposts, so you must draw your lines carefully to reduce their influence.

Reclaim the greatest share of the queen's desired lands and you will be declared the greatest cartographer in the kingdom!


Overview

In Cartographers, your goal is to earn as many points as you can by the time three seasons have passed.


How To Play

Each season you will place terrain pieces on the map until there are no pieces remaining this season, at which point the season is scored.

There are seven terrain types in Cartographers: Forests (green), Villages (red), Farms (yellow), Rivers (blue), Abysses (black), Mountains (brown and grey), and Monsters (purple).

Monster spaces will appear in random locations whenever an ambush occurs. You may also see orange spaces. These occur where your current terrain piece is overlapping with a filled space on the map. A piece can only be placed if it does not overlap any filled spaces.


Scoring

At the end of each season you will earn or lose points from several sources:

You will gain points based on the score card(s) corresponding to the season.

When scoring, adjacency is only determined cardinally and a cluster is a group of connected spaces that all have the same terrain type.

You will gain points equal to the number of coins you currently have. Coins persist from season to season.

You can gain coins by claiming mountains and placing some pieces. Mountains can be claimed by filling the four cardinally adjacent spaces

You will lose points for every empty space that is cardinally adjacent to at least one monster space on the map.

That's it for the rules! Now go and play some games!
